Cybersecurity Glossary

A definitive guide to the most common terms used in modern security. Understanding the language is the first step toward defense.

Simple Definition

Deceptive digital communications (email, SMS, web) designed to trick you into sharing sensitive data or downloading malware.

Red Flags

  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Generic greetings (e.g., "Dear Customer")
  • Misspelled URLs or sender addresses

Real-world Examples

Personal

An email claiming your Netflix account is suspended, asking you to update payment details immediately.

Work

A "CEO" request via email to purchase gift cards for a company event and send the codes back.

Simple Definition

A variation of phishing that uses SMS text messages as the attack vector to lure victims into revealing information.

Red Flags

  • Messages from unknown short-code numbers
  • Requests to click shortened links (e.g., bit.ly)
  • Claims of prize wins or missed deliveries

Real-world Examples

Personal

A text saying: "Your USPS package is held at our warehouse. Click here to confirm your address."

Work

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) fatigue: getting repeated "Approval" texts for logins you didn't start.

Simple Definition

The art of manipulating people so they give up confidential information or perform actions that compromise security.

Red Flags

  • Flattery or feigned friendship
  • Pretending to have authority (police, IT manager)
  • Creating a high-stress "emergency" scenario

Real-world Examples

Personal

A "friend" on social media messaging you for money because they are "stranded on vacation."

Work

"Tailgating": someone following you through a secure door while holding a coffee to look busy.
